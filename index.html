About Dana

She Didn't Study the Problem.
She Lived It.

Dana Kirchmar is a 39-year aviation and aerospace veteran who has held technical and executive roles at Lockheed Martin, Honeywell, and Mission Control. She has navigated every level of a male-dominated industry — from the operations floor to the boardroom — and built a career defined by technical credibility, not by waiting to be recognized for it.

Her work now translates that experience into two things: a framework that helps women in aviation stop proving themselves and start advancing, and a leadership development program that helps aviation executives understand what the talent retention gap is actually costing their operations. She speaks with the authority of someone who has been in the room — and the precision of someone who finally understands what was happening there.

Dana has spoken at Women in Aviation International, Aircraft Interiors Expo, and the Rocky Mountain AIAA Conference. She is the creator of NAVIGATE™ and ELEVATE™, programs designed for the professionals and leaders who are ready to stop diagnosing the problem and start solving it.

Signature Talks

Talks That Move Rooms,
Not Just Audiences

Each talk is built from thirty-nine years of operational experience. No motivational fluff. No outsider analysis. Just a peer who has been in the room longer — and knows where the traps are.

Keynote  ·  45–60 min

The Navigation Problem: Why Your Best Women Leaders Are Still Proving Themselves

Most high-performing women in aviation don't have a confidence problem. They have a navigation problem — and those require different solutions. This keynote reframes career friction as a structural, diagnosable challenge and gives audiences a precise vocabulary and a practical framework for what to do next.

Key Takeaways

  • Why framing career friction as a confidence deficit misdiagnoses the problem — and slows women down
  • The three structural patterns that derail high performers in technical aviation environments
  • A practical navigation framework for the next career decision in the room

Keynote  ·  Leadership Audiences  ·  45–60 min

The Cost of the Empty Seat: What Technical Talent Attrition Is Actually Doing to Your Operation

Aviation has a technical talent retention problem, and it disproportionately affects its women. Designed for leadership audiences — male and female — this talk translates the systemic problem into the operational and financial language executives actually use. No values case. No DEI framing. A business problem, presented by a peer.

Key Takeaways

  • The real cost of attrition in technical aviation roles — and why the standard numbers undercount it
  • What retention failures look like in MRO, OEM, and airline operations specifically
  • Three leadership behaviors that measurably change outcomes — with a specific action for Monday morning

Keynote  ·  General / Mixed Audience  ·  60 min

39 Years in the Hangar: What Aviation's Leadership Gap Looks Like From the Inside

A narrative keynote for general aviation audiences. Dana walks through four decades as the only woman in the room — and what she learned about the unwritten rules, the quiet exits, and the difference between surviving and actually building a career. This talk leaves every person in the room with something they can do differently.

Key Takeaways

  • The hidden rules that govern advancement in male-dominated technical environments
  • What the industry's most successful women do differently — and what it cost them to figure it out
  • A direct, honest message to the next generation entering aviation

Workshop  ·  Half Day

NAVIGATE™: A Career Operations Framework for Women in Aviation

A hands-on half-day workshop for women aviation professionals. Not a mindset session. A working session. Participants leave with a personalized career navigation map, a diagnosis of their specific friction points, and a 90-day action plan they can execute without anyone's permission.

What Participants Build

  • A written diagnosis of the structural barriers currently in their path
  • A career navigation map tailored to their specific role and environment
  • A 90-day action plan — three moves, no fluff, no waiting for the organization to change first
