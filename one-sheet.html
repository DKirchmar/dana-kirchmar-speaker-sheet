About Dana

Dana Kirchmar is a 39-year aviation and aerospace veteran with technical and executive experience across defense, business aviation, and connectivity. She began at Lockheed Martin Space Systems with a DoD Top Secret/SCI clearance supporting Space Shuttle and Titan IV launches, then co-designed the Citation X and Citation Excel autopilot at Honeywell and logged 100+ hours as a flight test engineer. Executive roles followed at Aviation Technology Group, Gogo Business Aviation, Ulterius Aviation, and SmartSky Networks. In 2021 she founded The Elevate Initiative to close aviation's leadership gap. She serves on the boards of Women in Aviation International, Wings Over the Rockies (Treasurer), and the Captain Jeppesen Foundation, and was named Aviation Week Laureate and Robb Report "Best in Aviation" in 2024.

Signature Talks

Keynote · Women Aviation Professionals · 45–60 min

The Navigation Problem: Why Your Best Women Leaders Are Still Proving Themselves

High-performing women in aviation aren't stuck because of talent — they're stuck because they're operating without the right map. This keynote reframes career friction as a structural, diagnosable challenge and introduces the NAVIGATE™ framework: eight disciplines that turn stalled momentum into forward motion.

Keynote · Aviation Leadership & Executives · 45–60 min

The Cost of the Empty Seat: What Technical Talent Attrition Is Actually Doing to Your Operation

When technical talent walks out the door, it shows up in safety margins, project timelines, and institutional knowledge that cannot be recovered. This keynote translates the women's leadership gap into operational and financial language that aviation executives recognize — and can act on.

Keynote · General / Mixed Audience · 60 min

39 Years in the Hangar: What Aviation's Leadership Gap Looks Like From the Inside

Spacecraft integrations. Autopilot certifications. Satellite networks. Four decades at the intersection of technical excellence and executive leadership — and the clearest possible view of what the industry's talent gap actually costs everyone in the room.

Workshop · Half Day

NAVIGATE™: A Career Operations Framework for Women in Aviation

Participants diagnose their career friction, map their NAVIGATE™ profile, and leave with a 90-day action plan built around eight disciplines: Non-Negotiables, Authenticity, Voice, Intuition, Guts, Authority, T-30, and Evolution.
